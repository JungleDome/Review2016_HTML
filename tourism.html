TOP 10 PLACES TO VISIT IN 2016

TOP 1
Bocas del Toro,Panama

Bocas del Toro is in Panama comprising an island chain off the Caribbean coast, and an addition of the biodiverse rainforest nearby the mainland. The area of Bocas del Toro is 1789 square miles. It contains seven islands, including Isla Colon, Isla Carenero, Isla Bastimentos. Its main island, Isla Colon is home to the capital, Bocas Town. The place is favorited by English-speaking and other tourists that travel to this Central American country. For those tourists that like adventure, the people at there has provided many activities to keep the busy, such as scuba diving, snorkeling, sailing, surfing and hiking are strongly suggested. There are places to visit and what to do list in Bocas del Toro.

TOP 2
Guadalajara, Mexico

Guadalajara is Mexico’s second biggest city. It is the capital of the state of Jalisco. (Board, 2012)This Mexican city is more about architecture, music and art scene. Not only cultural heritage, the historical aura is also exist in this inland city. Museums, parks and churches flourish alongside bullfights, rodeos and football. (TripAdvisor, 2017) Jazz music flows in the area while nearby area there are towns of Tequila, the famous drink that shares the same name is produced. It sometimes referred as Mexico’s Silicon Valley. Besides that, Tlaquepaque and Tonala contains Mexican artisans that create a selection of handcrafted items. Its famous places included Guadalajara Aquarium, Tapalpa, Mazamilta and more.

Places to visit

TOP 3
Richmond, Virginia

Richmond is the capital of the Commonwealth of Virginia in the United states. It was also the seat of America’s first African-American governor and the site of the first commercial electric streetcar system. (TripAdvisor, 2017) It is located at the fall line of the James River. This Southern city has been drawing history buffs for years, but nowadays with the addition of more art galleries and shopping destinations such as Carytown.

TOP 4
Lille, France

As everyone know, Paris is about fashion and design, but there is a charming town is making its mark on the map and society. This place is an architectural feast with strong Flemish influences: from the Grand Place to the basilica of Norte-Dame-de-la-Treille to the quiet streets of row houses and gardens. It may be France’s most underrated major city and home to the Musee d’Art Moderne and the Palais des Beax Arts. Kids will like the Parc de Zoologique. In addition, savvy travelers know that this place has a fine public transport system, making this location a pleasure to visit.

TOP 5
Iran

The landscapes of Iran are wonderful, from high mountains to deep deserts where the soulful presence of Asiatic cheetah still roams. Iran is also a treasure house for some of the beautiful architecture on the planet. Its historical commitment to aesthetic beauty and exquisite architecture resign supreme.

TOP 6
Lanai, Hawaii

It is almost a given fact that a Hawaiian destination would make the list, but this place, Lanai is truly an escape even in paradise. Its red-dirt island is an oasis of calm and peaceful. Among Hawaii’s six main islands, Lanai is one of the smallest populations and the least number of visitors. Reaching Lanai is difficult since there are no direct flights from the mainland to there. However, once people arrive, they will find the tropical surroundings are perfect for outdoor activities such as snorkeling and scuba diving.

TOP 7
Hangzhou, China

Hangzhou is the capital of Zhejiang Province and one of the seven ancient capitals of China. It has described by Italian traveler Marco Polo as the finest and most luxurious city in the world. Nowadays, it is one of the wealthiest cities in China. Hangzhou’s dreamy West Lake panoramas and beautiful green hills can easily tempt the tourists into long sojourns. Besides that, Hangzhou’s charm are by no means limited to West Lake scenery, it has further ancient pagodas and blissfully quiet temples at there.

TOP 8
Douro Valley, Portugal

Douro Valley is the world’s first officially designated wine region. A visit here is filled with rolling hills and number of wineries serving up some sensational sips. Tourists are suggested to come for the ports and wines, winding scenic roads, postcard-pretty villages and excellent regional restaurants.

TOP 9

It is an unexpectedly traditional and charming city, with half-timbered buildings huddled in its quaint medieval Altstadt, cozy apple wine taverns serving hearty regional food, village-like neighborhoods filled with outdoor cafes, boutiques and street art, and beautiful parks, gardens and riverside paths. This city is expanding its horizons with the openings of foodie-worthy restaurants.

TOP 10
Asbury Park, New Jersey

During decades of economic stagnation, the town of Asbury Park had nothing more to its name than the fact that state troubadour Bruce Springsteen got his start at the Stone Pony nightclub here in the mid-1970s. But since 2000, blocks of previously abandoned Victorian homes have seen such a revival that Asbury is sometimes called the Brooklyn of New Jersey. The downtown, several blocks of Cookman and Bangs Aves, has antiques shops, hip restaurants (from vegan to French bistro) and bars, and an art-house cinema.
